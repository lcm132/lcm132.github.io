Pilipino Association for Health Careers
To serve as support group for those who are interested in health careers and to educate the community of health-related issues relative to Pilipinos and their extended communities.

PROGRAMS
Medical Mission Benefit Concer
Proceeds from MMBC go directly toward the annual Medical Mission to the Philippines, organized by the Philippine Medical Association of Southern California (PMASC). Each year PMASC chooses a rural province in the Philippines that is poverty stricken and lacks adequate medical attention and provides free health services to the area. These services range from major to minor surgery, dentistry, optometry, and many more. This January the medical mission volunteers and health professionals will be heading to Nasubu, Batangas, Philippines!
Minorities in Health Conference
In order to address the underrepresentation of minority health care providers and the lacking health services common in communities of color, the Asian American Pacific In Health Research Group (AAPIHRG), Black Students in Health Association (BSHA), the Chicanos/Latinos in Health Education (CHE), and the Pilipino Association for Health Careers (PAHC) of UC Berkeley annually host the Minorities in Health (MIH) Conference.
Internships
PAHC serves as a support group for those interested in health careers. The Fall 2016 Intern class will learn about health-related issues then educate the community at their Intern-Run General Meeting towards the end of the semester. By creating a tight network of passionate, brilliant, and dedicated students, PAHC hopes to be your home here at Cal.
